The process

Lead to Legacy

01
The warm handoff
We get everyone introduced right from the start. A group email or text with all parties CC'd and contact information exchanged. If you don't have a process for this, I'll give you a template to make it easy.
02
Initial loan consultation and pre-approval questionnaire
We schedule the initial loan consultation to go over the client's wants, needs, and goals. Optionally, the client can complete the pre-approval questionnaire beforehand so we make the most of our time together.
03
Pre-qualification and document request
I review the client's loan application, confirm the path to approval, and follow up with any questions. I collect documentation to validate income, assets, and anything else needed to get them to the finish line.
04
Pre-approval
Once the client is pre-approved, I issue the pre-approval letter with the agency sheet, a pre-approval prescription showing exactly what homes are in their budget, and a do's and don'ts PDF to keep the process on track.
05
Communication to contract
Most LOs go silent between pre-approval and contract. I don't. Every Wednesday I check in with both you and the client, run real numbers on any properties they're considering, and advise on loan options and offer strategy. When an offer is ready, I personally call the listing agent to introduce myself and establish confidence in the buyer.
06
Rate lock consultation
Once under contract, I prepare loan disclosures and review options to make sure everything still aligns with the client's original goals. We discuss rate lock strategy and timing, cover next steps (appraisal, insurance, inspections), and review the contract and all contingency dates so nothing gets missed.
07
Tuesday updates
Every Tuesday I deliver loan status updates to the client, the buyer's agent, and the listing agent. Early-week communication means any issues surface with time to resolve them. Updates cover:
  • Borrower and third-party new contract welcome
  • Pre-underwriting updates
  • Loan submitted updates
  • Post-underwriting conditional approval
  • Initial and final closing disclosure
  • Borrower and third-party clear to close
08
Closing day
The borrower signs their final documents. We announce the loan as funded. You get to hand over the keys.
09
Post-closing consultation
I don't disappear after funding. I confirm their monthly payment, escrows, and first payment date, walk them through homestead tax exemptions, and warn them about the wave of junk mail coming their way as new homeowners. We revisit long-term financial goals and connect them with a financial planner if they don't have one yet. And if the client had a great experience, we ask for Google reviews for myself, you, and the agent -- because growing our brands together is part of the process too.
10
Legacy: Forever Follow-Up 777
Every Thursday I look back 7 days for recent closings to conduct post-closing consultations, and forward 7 days for upcoming birthdays and mortgage anniversaries. These touchpoints become mortgage reviews to check in on refinance opportunities and evolving real estate needs. Your clients stay in my ecosystem, which means repeat business and referrals loop back to you too.
